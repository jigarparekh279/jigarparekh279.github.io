Research

As a researcher in the Sustainable and Energy-Efficient Aviation (SEÂ²A) Cluster of Excellence, I am currently developing advanced algorithms for robust design optimization of airplane wings using scientific machine learning and Bayesian optimization. These methods enable the design of more sustainable and energy-efficient aircraft, contributing to greener aviation technology.

I serve as one of the core developers of SMARTy (Surrogate Modeling for AeRo-data Toolbox in Python), where I lead the development of the Surrogate-Based Uncertainty Quantification (SBUQ) and Surrogate-Based Optimization (SBO) modules. This toolbox plays a pivotal role in research and development efforts, being widely adopted by DLR's academic and industrial collaborators.

During my PhD, I contributed to an NWO-funded project focused on developing uncertainty quantification methodologies for wind turbine simulations, accounting for various sources and types of uncertainty. Amid the surge in AI-driven research, I conducted a distinctive study that rigorously compared data-driven approaches with physics-based methods for wind energy applications, offering valuable insights into their respective strengths and limitations.

Through my research and open-source contributions, I aim to lower the barriers to entry for the implementation, dissemination, and adoption of complex computational models, fostering innovation and collaboration in scientific and engineering communities.
